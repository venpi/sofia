Sophia and Her Three Daughters

A Meditation

Sections
I. II. III. IV. V.

I.

A young man from the future came upon Sophia at the edge of a river.

“I have read many books,” he said. “I can explain and name many things. But when the page closes, something in me stays restless. I don’t feel wise.”

Sophia threw a stone into the water. They watched the ripples unfold and fall quiet.

Downstream, a fish startled. A bird dove. A branch trembled. Another stone fell from the bank.

New ripples.

Sophia stayed silent. The young man stared at the water for a long while. Then he looked up.

“Which stone caused which ripples?”

She rose. “Come. I have three daughters.”

II.

In a field, a woman knelt in the soil and planted seeds. Her hands were rough. She was smiling.

“My daughter Faith,” said Sophia.

The young man watched. “How does she know they’ll grow?”

Faith held out a seed to him. He took it. Tiny, dry, almost lifeless.

She returned to her work.

He stood there, the seed in his palm. It was so small it could hide under his thumbnail. After a while, he knelt and pressed it into the earth. This felt irrational, yet his knees had already found the ground. He didn’t know why. But his hands did.

When he looked up, Faith was still smiling.

◆  ◆  ◆

III.

A woman watched the sunset from the window of a house. Orange flowed into purple.

“My daughter Hope,” said Sophia.

“What is she looking at?”

“Ask her.”

He approached. “What do you see?”

Hope didn’t turn. “I don’t know yet.”

The sun sank lower. The colors deepened. He found himself watching too.

“The sun looks like it’s dying,” he said.

Hope said nothing. They watched until the last light disappeared.

In the darkness, she finally spoke. “Stay until morning,” she said. “Some truths won’t come when you call them.”

◆  ◆  ◆

IV.

In a moonlit garden, a woman sat among flowers. She sat and breathed. That was all.

“My daughter Love,” said Sophia.

The young man hesitated. Love looked so at ease that he worried he’d bring noise into the garden.

She opened her eyes, smiled, and patted the ground beside her.

He sat.

A moth moved from flower to flower. An owl called. Another answered. The moon rose higher.

He realized he had stopped thinking about the past. He had stopped rehearsing the future. He was just here. This garden. This moment.

Love picked a flower and handed it to him. Its petals were soft against his fingers.

Neither spoke. There was no need to say anything.

◆  ◆  ◆

V.

They returned to the river. The water was dark now, reflecting stars. Two skies: one above, one below.

Sophia picked up three stones and dropped them, one by one. The ripples spread, met, and made patterns no single stone could make alone.

The young man watched.

“I think I understand,” he said slowly. “Faith trusts the process. Hope stays open to what it might bring. Love participates in it fully.”

Sophia waited.

“And you,” he began, then paused. “You help me see the process itself.”

She said nothing. But something in her silence felt like a yes.

He picked up a stone, turned it in his fingers. “I came here looking for answers outside the process.”

He dropped it in the water.

“But I’m in the river too. Aren’t I?”

Dawn was breaking. The sky turned from black to blue, from blue to gold.

When he turned, Sophia was gone.

Or perhaps she was everywhere: in the light on the water, in the hush between his thoughts, in the feeling of being alive on a new morning.

He stayed by the river a while longer, watching the ripples fade.

Then he rose and walked back into the world.
